# Conceptual frame

## The present being mined

The project begins from a simple reversal:

```txt
The machine does not produce the future.
It reveals the present being mined.
```

AI is not treated as a floating intelligence. It is treated as an apparatus bound to extractive infrastructures: rare earths, data centers, energy grids, water, labor, networks, supply chains, and e-waste.

## Archive as future residue

The printer does not produce a clean screen output. It produces residue: paper, ink, noise, misalignment, mechanical rhythm, storage problems, and physical accumulation.

The continuous paper is an archive in formation. It is not just documentation of the work. It is one of the material forms of the work.

## Obsolete media as active matter

The dot-matrix printer is not used as nostalgia. It acts as a bureaucratic and institutional machine, a device that historically belongs to forms, ledgers, receipts, reports, and administrative truth.

In this project, that administrative voice is redirected toward a speculative archive of ecological and computational collapse.

## E-waste and refusal

The hardware body of the installation should not hide computation behind a clean black box. The system may expose both living and dead hardware: controller, rack, cables, switches, drives, memory, power supplies, and electronic remains.

This produces a tension between operational machine and electronic ruin.

## AI as phármakon

AI appears as remedy and poison: a tool for sensing, writing, and connecting, but also a system dependent on the same extractive regimes it helps describe.

The installation should not reconcile this contradiction too quickly.
