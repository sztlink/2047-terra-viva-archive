# Peer field

This file maps public affinities and adjacent practices for **Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva**.

It does not document private collaboration, assign influence, or define the final form of the artwork.

## Purpose of this file

The project sits near several fields at once:

- live information systems;
- printer and paper-based apparatuses;
- archive politics and counter-archives;
- media archaeology and obsolete media;
- e-waste and material computation;
- AI as phármakon;
- extractive computation and planetary infrastructure;
- generative fiction and machine writing.

This file helps situate the work without reducing it to a single lineage.

## Not influences, not a closed bibliography

The entries below are not presented as direct influences unless explicitly stated elsewhere.

They are better understood as:

- apparatus relatives;
- shared fields;
- productive distances;
- public vocabularies;
- adjacent practices.

The goal is not to prove originality through comparison. The goal is to make the project's field legible.

## Co-authorship and public boundaries

**Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva** is an artwork in development by Giselle Beiguelman and Felipe Sztutman.

Because Beiguelman is a co-author, she should not appear here as a normal external reference. Instead, this repository can acknowledge the public research fields associated with her work: archive politics, image politics, digital ruins, technological memory, artificial intelligence as phármakon, and the material afterlives of computational culture.

This repository does not document the private co-authorial process. It only maps public research affinities and the technical-artistic apparatus layer maintained by Felipe Sztutman.

## Apparatus relatives: live information, printers, institutional systems

### Hans Haacke - *News* (1969 / 2008)

**Reference:** [SFMOMA collection record](https://www.sfmoma.org/artwork/2008.232/)

**Relation:** live information feed, printer, paper accumulation, institutional time.

**Difference:** *News* foregrounds information flow, media systems, and institutional critique. Projeto 2047 shifts the apparatus toward extractive computation, AI, e-waste, rare earths, and a future archive.

**Why it matters here:** the printer is not only an output device. It is a memory machine that turns external signals into physical residue.

*News* is a close apparatus-relative, not a total conceptual ancestor.

### Mark Hansen and Ben Rubin - *Listening Post* (2002)

**Reference:** [Ben Rubin / EAR project page](https://www.earstudio.com/projects/project-page/listening-post)

**Relation:** live textual signals, networked speech, public display, data as chorus.

**Difference:** *Listening Post* turns online language into a spatial audiovisual field. Projeto 2047 routes public signals toward speculative archive, mechanical inscription, and paper residue.

**Why it matters here:** live data can become more than information. It can become rhythm, voice, atmosphere, and collective trace.

### RYBN

**Relation:** algorithmic systems, finance, networks, machines operating in public view.

**Difference:** RYBN often foregrounds autonomous systems and economic infrastructures. Projeto 2047 focuses on extractive computation, environmental future, obsolete output, and archive formation.

**Why it matters here:** systems can be staged not as interfaces, but as operating apparatuses.

### Forensic Architecture

**Relation:** evidence, spatial reconstruction, data, testimony, political infrastructure.

**Difference:** Forensic Architecture works toward evidentiary claims in legal and political contexts. Projeto 2047 works through speculative archive and installation, where evidence and fiction remain in productive tension.

**Why it matters here:** the archive is never neutral. Evidence is constructed through media, method, and institutional frame.

### Bureau d'Études

**Relation:** diagrams of power, institutions, networks, corporations, and infrastructure.

**Difference:** Bureau d'Études maps existing systems of power. Projeto 2047 may include diagrams and apparatuses, but its central form is a future archive generated from present extraction.

**Why it matters here:** technical diagrams can be political images.

## Archive, memory, counter-archive

### Giselle Beiguelman - public research fields

**Relation:** image politics, archive politics, digital ruins, technological memory, AI as phármakon.

**Difference:** This is not an external reference. It is part of the co-authored field of the work.

**Why it matters here:** Projeto 2047 is inseparable from questions of memory, archive, technological ruin, image politics, and the poison / remedy ambiguity of AI.

### Hito Steyerl

**Relation:** poor images, circulation, infrastructure, violence, visibility, and power.

**Difference:** Steyerl's work often follows the politics of images through networks, militarization, and circulation. Projeto 2047 turns toward mechanical inscription, e-waste, and future archive.

**Why it matters here:** images are not only representations. They are infrastructures, labor, compression, violence, and circulation.

### Walid Raad / The Atlas Group

**Reference:** [The Atlas Group archive](https://www.theatlasgroup1989.org/)

**Relation:** archive as fiction, counter-history, institutional memory, constructed evidence.

**Difference:** The Atlas Group stages fictional archives around historical trauma and political memory. Projeto 2047 uses speculative future archive to expose a present already being mined.

**Why it matters here:** fiction can operate as an archival method, not an escape from reality.

### Susan Schuppli

**Reference:** [*Material Witness* project page](https://susanschuppli.com/MATERIAL-WITNESS) and [MIT Press book page](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262043571/material-witness/)

**Relation:** material evidence, toxic media, environmental testimony, forensic matter.

**Difference:** Schuppli's work examines how materials bear witness. Projeto 2047 asks how paper, printer, e-waste, and AI traces can become a speculative witness-machine.

**Why it matters here:** matter can testify.

### Mimi Onuoha

**Reference:** [*The Library of Missing Datasets*](https://mimi-onuoha-9s0o.squarespace.com/the-library-of-missing-datasets) and [Missing Datasets repository](https://github.com/MimiOnuoha/missing-datasets)

**Relation:** missing datasets, absence, classification, data politics.

**Difference:** Onuoha's work foregrounds absence in datasets and social systems. Projeto 2047 may use external signals, but must also attend to what extraction makes invisible.

**Why it matters here:** an archive is shaped as much by absence as by accumulation.

## Media archaeology, obsolete media, and digital ruins

### Jussi Parikka

**Reference:** [*A Geology of Media*, University of Minnesota Press](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9780816695522/a-geology-of-media/)

**Relation:** media geology, minerals, waste, ecology of media, deep material infrastructures.

**Difference:** Parikka provides a theoretical field rather than an apparatus model. Projeto 2047 turns these questions into a live installation body.

**Why it matters here:** media history is also geological history.

### Wolfgang Ernst

**Relation:** media archaeology, operational time, archives as technical systems.

**Difference:** Ernst's work helps think time and archive through machines. Projeto 2047 stages a machine that produces archival time through continuous output.

**Why it matters here:** machines do not only store memory. They produce temporal regimes.

### Siegfried Zielinski

**Relation:** variantology, deep time of media, non-linear histories of technical apparatuses.

**Difference:** Zielinski opens media history beyond progress narratives. Projeto 2047 similarly refuses the idea that newer media simply replace older media.

**Why it matters here:** obsolete media can return as active matter, not as nostalgia.

### Rosa Menkman

**Relation:** glitch, compression, signal failure, artifacts of digital systems.

**Difference:** Projeto 2047 is not primarily a glitch work, but printer errors, encoding failures, and mechanical instability may become material evidence.

**Why it matters here:** failure can reveal the system, but it should not become decoration.

### Olia Lialina and Dragan Espenschied

**Relation:** vernacular web, preservation, digital folklore, web history.

**Difference:** Their work around web memory and preservation differs from the printer apparatus, but shares concern with technological cultures that disappear too quickly.

**Why it matters here:** preservation includes aesthetics, habits, formats, interfaces, and everyday technical culture.

## Extractive computation and planetary infrastructure

### Kate Crawford and Vladan Joler - *Anatomy of an AI System* (2018)

**Reference:** [Official project site](https://anatomyof.ai/)

**Relation:** AI supply chains, labor, minerals, logistics, planetary extraction.

**Difference:** *Anatomy of an AI System* diagrams the hidden material infrastructures behind a consumer AI device. Projeto 2047 turns extractive computation into a speculative archive apparatus.

**Why it matters here:** AI is not immaterial. It is geological, logistical, energetic, labor-intensive, and political.

### Joana Moll

**Reference:** [*CO2GLE* (2014)](https://www.janavirgin.com/co2gle.html)

**Relation:** materiality of networks, energy, surveillance, platform infrastructures.

**Difference:** Moll often exposes the energy and political costs of digital systems. Projeto 2047 stages a machine that writes from within those costs.

**Why it matters here:** computation has a footprint before it has an image.

### Unknown Fields

**Reference:** [Unknown Fields Division](https://unknownfieldsdivision.com/) and [*Rare Earthenware*, V&A collection](https://collections.vam.ac.uk/item/O1326004/rare-earthenware-vessel-unknown-fields-division/)

**Relation:** supply chains, rare earths, material geographies, speculative fieldwork.

**Difference:** Unknown Fields often maps geopolitical and material routes of extraction. Projeto 2047 compresses those routes into an archive-producing installation.

**Why it matters here:** rare earths are not background. They are part of the image system.

### Ursula Biemann

**Relation:** ecologies, extraction, territory, video essay, environmental geopolitics.

**Difference:** Biemann's work often operates through essayistic moving image and field research. Projeto 2047 works through printer, paper, AI, and e-waste as apparatus.

**Why it matters here:** environmental futures are mediated through territory, technology, and image regimes.

### World of Matter

**Reference:** [World of Matter project site](http://worldofmatter.net/about-project)

**Relation:** resources, extraction, infrastructure, political ecology, visual research.

**Difference:** World of Matter maps resource ecologies through research and media. Projeto 2047 creates a speculative archive machine from related concerns.

**Why it matters here:** resource extraction is a media condition.

## AI as phármakon and synthetic memory

### Giselle Beiguelman - AI as phármakon

**Relation:** AI as remedy and poison, image politics, synthetic visual regimes.

**Difference:** Again, this is not an outside influence. It belongs to the shared co-authorial and public research field of the project.

**Why it matters here:** the system should not present AI as clean solution. It is both instrument and symptom.

### Trevor Paglen

**Reference:** [*ImageNet Roulette* project note](https://paglen.studio/2020/04/29/imagenet-roulette/)

**Relation:** machine vision, datasets, surveillance, invisible infrastructures of seeing.

**Difference:** Paglen often interrogates visual systems of power and military / surveillance infrastructures. Projeto 2047 approaches AI through archive, extraction, printer output, and e-waste.

**Why it matters here:** machine images are political before they are aesthetic.

### Stephanie Dinkins

**Relation:** AI, memory, conversation, ancestry, community, and care.

**Difference:** Dinkins foregrounds relational and intergenerational dimensions of AI. Projeto 2047 works through speculative environmental archive and material apparatus.

**Why it matters here:** AI memory is not only technical. It is social, situated, and ethically charged.

### Holly Herndon and Mat Dryhurst

**Relation:** model training, authorship, voice, dataset politics, consent.

**Difference:** Their work foregrounds voice, model ownership, and cultural production in the age of AI. Projeto 2047 uses AI as part of an apparatus of future archive and material critique.

**Why it matters here:** synthetic output is always entangled with authorship, training, consent, and mediation.

## Bureaucratic print, seriality, and paper residue

This field is less about a single artist and more about a material culture:

- receipts;
- forms;
- continuous paper;
- ledgers;
- audit trails;
- logs;
- reports;
- dot-matrix invoices;
- administrative truth.

The printer in Projeto 2047 inherits this bureaucratic memory and redirects it toward speculative environmental testimony.

### On Kawara

**Relation:** date, seriality, time, repetition, record.

**Difference:** Kawara is a distant temporal relative, not an apparatus relative. Projeto 2047 is machine-generated, infrastructure-bound, and materially unstable.

**Why it matters here:** repetition can turn time into evidence.

### Hanne Darboven

**Relation:** serial inscription, time systems, writing, accumulation.

**Difference:** Darboven's serial systems are not printer systems, but they help situate paper-based accumulation as conceptual time.

**Why it matters here:** the archive can be built through repetitive inscription.

## Productive distances

### Refik Anadol

**Relation:** AI, data, large-scale immersive display.

**Distance:** Projeto 2047 should not be understood as data spectacle. Its emphasis is not smooth immersion, but friction, residue, extraction, obsolete output, and archive politics.

**Why include the distance:** public AI art is often read through spectacle. This project needs another vocabulary.

### Generic cyberpunk scenography

**Relation:** visible cables, machines, ruins, future aesthetics.

**Distance:** Projeto 2047 should avoid using e-waste as generic cyberpunk decoration. Hardware remains should function as evidence, not atmosphere only.

### Sustainability illustration

**Relation:** ecological themes, climate futures, regeneration, crisis.

**Distance:** Projeto 2047 is not an illustration of sustainability topics. It is an apparatus that stages the contradiction between computational futures and extractive presents.

## Working position

Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva combines:

```txt
speculative future archive
+ dot-matrix inscription
+ e-waste / hardware body
+ AI as extractive apparatus
+ public / private archive politics
```

Its field is not only AI art. It is media archaeology under extractive computation.
