# Project brief

**Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva** is an artwork in development around a speculative near future in which rare earth extraction, automated governance, environmental collapse, and computational infrastructures become inseparable.

The work treats 2047 not as distant science fiction, but as a compressed image of the present. The future is already being mined through data, minerals, energy, water, e-waste, and automated decision systems.

## Core gesture

A machine writes and prints a living archive.

The apparatus reads the present and generates fragments from 2047. These fragments are printed continuously by a dot-matrix printer on continuous paper, turning computation into noise, paper, residue, and institutional trace.

## Working formulation

```txt
Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva is a living archive of an avoidable catastrophe.
It is written by contemporary extraction, printed by obsolete media, and haunted by artificial intelligence.
```

## Material axes

- Rare earths and extractive futures.
- E-waste and urban mining.
- Obsolete media as active historical matter.
- AI as computational infrastructure, not immaterial magic.
- The archive as a site of resistance, not just preservation.
- Continuous paper as accumulation, residue, and evidence.

## Apparatus axes

- Dot-matrix printer as institutional voice and noisy witness.
- Local controller as the visible body of the system.
- Generative model as dramaturgical engine.
- News and public signals as present-tense inputs.
- Hardware waste as scenography, evidence, and material contradiction.

## Public archive purpose

This repository preserves the research process around the apparatus and system. It does not expose private negotiations, closed documents, or final curatorial decisions.
