# Dot-matrix printer

The dot-matrix printer is a central apparatus, not a nostalgic prop.

Current prototype: **Epson FX-2190** installed at AYA Studio. See `printer-research/` for recovered calibration notes, CUPS / ESC-P pipeline notes, continuous paper, encoding, and failure modes.

## Why dot-matrix

A dot-matrix printer carries bureaucratic memory. It belongs to invoices, reports, logs, forms, continuous paper, and administrative truth. Its sound is mechanical and public. Its output accumulates physically.

In this project, the printer becomes a witness that writes from 2047 while being physically anchored in older computational culture.

## Material properties

- Slow output.
- Audible impact.
- Continuous paper.
- Mechanical error.
- Misalignment and wear.
- Ink ribbon degradation.
- Accumulation over time.

These are not bugs by default. They are part of the material vocabulary of the work.

## Archive behavior

The printed paper should be treated as an archive in formation.

Possible modes:

- chronological accumulation;
- dated fragments;
- daily bundles;
- visible paper pile;
- staged storage boxes;
- marked failures and reprints;
- sections generated from different source conditions.

## Open questions

- Should the paper be readable during the exhibition, or mainly accumulate as evidence?
- Should fragments be dated by present time, fictional 2047 time, or both?
- Should printer failures remain visible?
- Should the sound be amplified, isolated, or left as raw mechanical noise?
