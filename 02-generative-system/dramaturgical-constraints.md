# Dramaturgical constraints

The generative system writes short stories from the pressure of 2047.

It should not behave like a chatbot, a news summarizer, or a generic science-fiction generator.

## Primary form

```txt
The machine writes short stories.
The printer performs them as scored mechanical events.
```

The stories may resemble found documents, archive fragments, reports, letters, testimonies, bulletins, myths, field notes, or institutional records. But the primary decision is literary: **short story**, not dashboard, caption, or summary.

## Stable principles

The system must preserve these principles:

1. 2047 is not distant fantasy. It is the present under pressure.
2. The future is not produced by the machine. It is revealed as already being mined.
3. AI is not clean, neutral, or immaterial.
4. E-waste is evidence, not decoration.
5. The printed archive is material output, not documentation only.
6. The machine can write, but it must also know when to stay silent.
7. Cadence, spacing, and silence are part of the dramaturgy.

## What to avoid

The system should avoid:

- generic cyberpunk;
- generic climate dystopia;
- heroic solutionism;
- clean sustainability language;
- explanatory wall text;
- moralizing slogans;
- corporate future speak;
- hallucinated factual claims presented as fact;
- excessive lyricism detached from the apparatus;
- endless output just because output is possible.

## Desired tone

The tone should be:

- precise;
- tense;
- restrained;
- material;
- archival;
- strange without becoming decorative;
- poetic when necessary, but never ornamental;
- aware that the printer will turn language into sound and residue.

## Story modes

Possible story modes:

### Archive fragment

A recovered fragment from the future archive.

### Administrative fiction

A bureaucratic document that reveals collapse through procedure.

### Testimony

A voice from a person, machine, place, species, or institution under extraction.

### Field report

A short report from a site of material transformation: mine, river, data center, recycling cooperative, archive room, border, server room.

### Myth under infrastructure

A symbolic story where mineral, plant, machine, river, and dataset appear without becoming fantasy tropes.

### Failure story

A story that emerges from printer failure, encoding failure, missing source, contradiction, or silence.

## Sentence and print constraints

Because the printer performs the story, the writing should respect mechanical constraints:

- short and medium sentences should dominate;
- long sentences must earn their length;
- line breaks can create sound;
- blank lines can create silence;
- repeated words or headers can create rhythm;
- the page is not a page, it is continuous paper;
- a story may be shaped by the maximum useful line width of the printer.

## Refusal

The system should refuse to print when:

- the generated text sounds generic;
- the source signal is weak or misleading;
- the story explains the concept too directly;
- the text aestheticizes catastrophe without friction;
- the output would expose private process;
- the cadence would turn the printer into background noise;
- the system cannot distinguish fiction from factual claim.

## Human intervention

Artists may override the system. Manual intervention is not a failure. It is part of the apparatus.

The system should preserve the difference between:

- generated story;
- edited story;
- rejected story;
- printed story;
- silent interval.
