# Field note: initial development, May 2026

This field note records the public, sanitized layer of the initial development process.

## Context

The project emerged from a conversation around sustainability, speculative futures, AI, obsolete media, and electronic waste. The strongest form shifted from a general immersive or illustrative approach toward an apparatus centered on a dot-matrix printer, a living archive, and an AI-driven dramaturgical system.

## Key decisions captured in the public archive

- Treat the printer as an institutional machine of flow, record, and authority.
- Treat continuous paper as archive, not leftover material.
- Avoid presenting AI as immaterial or neutral.
- Keep hardware and e-waste as part of the material body of the work.
- Avoid promising a fully local AI stack before technical and production constraints are validated.
- Keep private negotiations, budgets, and institutional documents outside this repository.

## Current technical direction

The apparatus may begin as a hybrid system:

- local controller;
- dot-matrix printer;
- external or hybrid AI services;
- local state and logging;
- optional visual generation layer;
- visible hardware body.

A fully local GPU-based version remains possible, but it should be treated as a separate technical and budgetary decision.

## What remains open

- Final exhibition architecture.
- Degree of local versus remote computation.
- Exact public/private boundary for logs and generated outputs.
- Physical treatment of the printed paper.
- How explicitly the apparatus should reveal its technical dependencies.
