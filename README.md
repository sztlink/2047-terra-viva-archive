# Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva

**Technical-artistic research archive**  
Giselle Beiguelman and Felipe Sztutman

This repository documents the public, sanitized research layer of **Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva**, an artwork in development around artificial intelligence, extractive futures, rare earths, e-waste, obsolete media, and the material politics of computation.

The repository focuses on Felipe Sztutman's technical-artistic process: the installation apparatus, generative system, dot-matrix printer archive, hardware and e-waste scenography, and archive policy.

## Status

Preliminary research archive.  
Not an official institutional repository.  
Not an exhibition announcement.  
Not a production dossier.

Live command center prototype:

- <https://sztlink.github.io/2047-terra-viva-archive/06-prototypes/command-center/>

Materials here are partial, redacted, and versioned as field notes. They are meant to preserve process without exposing private conversations, institutional negotiations, budgets, or closed documents.

## Short thesis

```txt
The machine does not produce the future.
It reveals the present being mined.
```

## What this repository is

- A public research archive for the technical and material development of the work.
- A notebook for the apparatus: printer, controller, generative pipeline, paper archive, and hardware body.
- A place to document references, diagrams, and decisions that can survive beyond a single exhibition context.
- A boundary object between artistic practice, media archaeology, AI systems, and archive politics.

## What this repository is not

- Not the full artwork.
- Not an official institutional press kit.
- Not a budget or procurement folder.
- Not a dump of private process.
- Not a publication of internal e-mails, WhatsApp messages, presentations, or closed curatorial material.
- Not a claim that the work is technically or conceptually fixed.

## Authorship and scope

**Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva** is an artwork in development by Giselle Beiguelman and Felipe Sztutman.

This repository is maintained by Felipe Sztutman as a public research archive for his technical-artistic layer of the process. The repository does not replace shared authorship, curatorial framing, or future institutional materials.

## Public and private layers

The project has several layers of visibility:

- **Private process:** conversations, drafts, budgets, internal documents, production details.
- **Research archive:** sanitized public notes, system diagrams, references, apparatus documentation.
- **Artwork and exhibition:** the final form, to be defined through the artistic and institutional process.

Only the research archive layer belongs here.
