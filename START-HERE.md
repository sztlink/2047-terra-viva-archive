# Start here

If you have five minutes, start here.

## What this is

**Projeto 2047 / Arquivo Terra Viva** is an artwork in development by Giselle Beiguelman and Felipe Sztutman.

This repository is the public, sanitized research archive for Felipe Sztutman's technical-artistic layer of the process: apparatus, printer, generative system, hardware body, e-waste, and archive policy.

It is not an official institutional repository, not a press release, and not a production folder.

## Short thesis

```txt
The machine does not produce the future.
It reveals the present being mined.
```

The project treats AI as material infrastructure, not immaterial intelligence. It asks how extractive computation, rare earths, e-waste, obsolete media, and archive politics can become an installation apparatus.

## The apparatus in one paragraph

A system reads signals from the present, processes them through a constrained generative engine, and prints fragments from 2047 through a dot-matrix printer on continuous paper. The printed paper becomes a living archive. Around the printer, the hardware body may expose both living computation and electronic remains: controller, rack, cables, switches, drives, memory, and e-waste.

## Why a dot-matrix printer

The printer is not nostalgia. It is a bureaucratic machine of flow, record, noise, and institutional truth. In this work, it becomes a witness: a device that prints a future already embedded in the material conditions of the present.

## Why e-waste

The project refuses the fantasy that computation is clean or immaterial. AI depends on energy, water, minerals, supply chains, data centers, networks, and discarded hardware. E-waste is not decoration here. It is evidence.
